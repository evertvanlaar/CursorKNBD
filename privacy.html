Privacy policy

Last updated: 2 May 2026 · Applies to kalanera.gr (website and installable PWA). This notice reflects how the published frontend and backends behave today.

1. Who we are

The Kala Nera Business Directory website and mobile-style web app (“PWA”) are operated for local discovery of businesses and related services around Kala Nera, Pelion, Greece (project “E‑Project”). For privacy questions contact info@spiti.tech.

2. What this covers

This policy describes categories of data processing tied to features actually shipped in the current site/app bundle (including cached directories, bus timetable integration, lightweight telemetry, analytics scripts, offline behaviour, and links or embeds to third-party sites).

This document is informational and does not constitute legal advice.

3. Analytics & measurement (browser)

3.1 Google Analytics 4

Certain pages include Google Analytics 4, operated by Google. That service may use cookies or similar technologies and process usage-related data under Google’s policies. See Google’s privacy documentation for retention, regional controllers, and available controls.

3.2 GoatCounter

Pages that include the GoatCounter snippet send privacy‑oriented page-view statistics to stats.kalanera.gr (script hosted by GoatCounter). Connection metadata is handled under GoatCounter’s terms.

4. Application telemetry (POST to automation backend)

After load, the client sends a small JSON payload (typically ~1.5 s delay) via HTTPS POST to a webhook endpoint on self-hosted workflow automation (n8n) operated for this site (path ends with /webhook/app-stats). We intentionally do not print the full hostname in this public notice to avoid unnecessary disclosure; it is visible in the published JavaScript bundle and in browser developer tools (Network tab). For regulatory or data-protection enquiries about processors, contact info@spiti.tech. Fields match the live frontend implementation:

  • event: one of app_open, installatie (first recorded version), update (bundle version changed)
  • source: App (PWA) vs Website (Browser) (standalone display-mode detection)
  • version / css_version: application and stylesheet cache-bust markers
  • os / device: coarse flags derived from the user-agent (e.g. Android / iOS / Desktop, Mobile vs Desktop)
  • screen: viewport width×height at send time
  • referrer: document.referrer or “direct”
  • theme: light/dark inferred from prefers-color-scheme
  • datum: human-readable timestamp string (locale NL in the shipped script)

Automation on the server may combine the request IP (as seen by the webhook) with third-party geo-lookup tools (for example IP-API style services) for aggregated, non-precise location context. Purpose: operations, adoption, and debugging — not profiling for advertising on our side.

The published frontend skips sending this telemetry POST while you stay on privacy.html or privacy-el.html.

5. Directory & timetable data (GET webhooks)

Published business listings and bus timetable data are loaded with ordinary HTTPS GET requests to separate webhook endpoints on the same automation backend (paths include /webhook/local-businesses and /webhook/bus-schedule-next; the bus endpoint uses query parameters for direction/day). As with any website, infrastructure may log technical data (IP address, TLS information, timestamps). The public site does not require a user login for viewing this information.

Server-side workflows may cache sheet snapshots briefly (configured TTL) to reduce Google Sheets/API load — that processing happens on automation infrastructure you operate, not in the visitor’s browser.

6. Weather widget

Where the weather chip is shown, the app calls Open‑Meteo (api.open-meteo.com) using fixed coordinates for Kala Nera. No precise device GPS is sent for that lookup. Review Open‑Meteo’s privacy statement for details.

7. Embedded content & outbound links

Pages may embed or link to external resources (e.g. live webcam images, Meteoblue, Google Maps, Facebook, KTEL websites). Opening those links or embeds exposes standard connection data to those third parties under their policies.

8. Local storage on your device

The app uses browser localStorage (and PWA caches via the service worker) to improve performance and offline use. Examples aligned with the current codebase include (non-exhaustive):

  • kalanera_offline_data / kalanera_last_sync — cached business directory snapshot and last refresh label
  • kalanera_list_mode — directory view mode preference
  • kalanera_wishlist — favourites you save in the browser (stays on device unless you later use a server feature that transmits it)
  • kalanera_bus_schedule_cache_v3, kalanera_bus_dir, kalanera_bus_day_offset — bus UI state and timetable cache slots
  • app_version — last reported app bundle version for telemetry event type
  • theme — dark/light preference

You can clear site data via browser or PWA settings; that removes these local copies.

9. Service worker (PWA)

A service worker caches static assets and may serve an offline banner. It reports its version string to the page for display/diagnostics. It does not replace the analytics/telemetry statements above.

10. Fonts & CDN

We load fonts from Google Fonts and icons from cdnjs (Font Awesome). Your browser connects to those CDNs; they may log IP and technical metadata under their policies.

11. Legal bases & rights (EEA/UK overview)

Where GDPR/UK GDPR applies, we rely on appropriate grounds such as legitimate interests (secure operation, aggregated statistics, product improvement) and, where required, consent for non-essential cookies or similar technologies — in line with national guidance (e.g. cookie rules for analytics). You may have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection; contact info@spiti.tech. You may also lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority.

12. Children

The directory is aimed at travellers and local services. It is not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect children’s personal data.

13. Changes

When features or processors change materially, we will update this page and the “Last updated” date.

This privacy page intentionally does not load the same analytics scripts as other marketing pages, to keep this read as lightweight as possible. If you navigate back to the main site, normal measurement may resume.
